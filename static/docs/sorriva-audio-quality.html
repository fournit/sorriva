Audio Quality Reference

What lossless means, what hi-res means, what each streaming service actually delivers, and what Sorriva delivers on each hardware path. Internal reference — feeds product spec and competitive matrix.

The concepts explained

Lossless and hi-res are two independent properties that get conflated constantly — even by the services themselves.

The important one
Lossless
Audio data is mathematically identical to the original recording. Nothing is thrown away during compression. FLAC and ALAC are lossless codecs. The difference between lossy (Spotify AAC) and lossless (Apple Music FLAC) is real, measurable, and audible on decent hardware. This is the line that matters most.
The debated one
Hi-Res Audio
Audio captured or stored at higher resolution than CD — 24-bit depth and/or sample rates above 44.1kHz (96kHz, 192kHz). Can also be lossless. Whether humans can reliably distinguish hi-res from lossless CD quality in blind tests is genuinely contested. Sorriva supports it where the architecture allows — but doesn't oversell it.
The baseline
CD Quality — 16-bit / 44.1kHz
The Red Book standard. Captures frequencies up to 22kHz (above human hearing). Dynamic range of 96dB. When encoded losslessly in FLAC or ALAC, this is bit-perfect to the CD master. Lossless CD quality is excellent audio — it already exceeds human perceptual limits in both dimensions. Not a compromise.
The nuance
Lossless ≠ Hi-Res
These are independent axes. A FLAC file at 16/44.1 is lossless but not hi-res. A 24/96 FLAC is both lossless and hi-res. Spotify at 320kbps AAC is neither — it's lossy. Apple Music FLAC at 16/44.1 is lossless but not hi-res. Qobuz at 24/192 is lossless and hi-res. The marketing conflates them constantly.

Quick reference — what the numbers mean

◈ Bit depth
Controls dynamic range — the difference between the quietest and loudest sound. 16-bit = 96dB range. 24-bit = 144dB range. Human hearing tops out at ~120dB. Both exceed real-world listening needs.
◈ Sample rate (kHz)
Controls frequency ceiling — the highest pitch captured. 44.1kHz captures up to 22kHz. Human hearing tops out at ~20kHz. Higher sample rates (96, 192kHz) capture frequencies above human hearing — theoretically irrelevant, debated in practice.
◈ The practical takeaway
Lossy → lossless is a meaningful, audible upgrade on good hardware. Lossless CD → lossless hi-res is subtle at best, inaudible to most. Sorriva's architecture delivers lossless everywhere and hi-res where the delivery path supports it.

On-device playback (local files)
iPhone / iPad → headphones

iPhone / iPad → AirPlay 2 zone
Wireless push via AirPlay 2
 16-bit / 44.1kHz ✓ Lossless CD In path AirPlay 2 downgrades local hi-res files to 16/44.1 in transit. Lossless CD quality — not lossy, not bit-perfect hi-res. Sorriva will surface this in the signal path indicator.
Sorriva's honest position

Sorriva delivers lossless audio on every path — the only exception is Spotify (which isn't lossless at the source) and mixed-hardware AirPlay 2 groups (which are still lossless CD quality, not lossy). Hi-res up to 24/192 is available via Qobuz Connect and Tidal Connect to Bluesound hardware, and via on-device playback of local files. The AirPlay 2 quality ceiling is a real constraint for mixed-hardware groups and local-file wireless delivery — Sorriva surfaces it honestly rather than hiding it.
