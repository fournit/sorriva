Roon

Audiophile Server · $130/yr

Roon is the gold standard for audiophile library management. It validates the market for what Sorriva is building — but its architecture (server-required, hardware-locked, mobile-as-afterthought) creates the exact openings Sorriva was designed to fill.

Hardware freedom
Works with any speaker/amplifier, not just a certified list
 ✗ Roon Ready only ↑ Open ecosystem Ours
Roon requires "Roon Ready" certification — a vendor-gated list that requires hardware manufacturers to license and implement RAAT. Sorriva works with any device supporting AirPlay 2, Spotify Connect, Qobuz Connect, or Tidal Connect — which covers essentially all serious audio hardware made in the last five years. No certification required. No ecosystem to join. "Any speaker. Any service. One app."
EQ & tone control
Per-room equalization and tone adjustment
 ✓ DSP engine ◎ EQService Theirs
Roon's DSP engine — parametric EQ, upsampling, room correction — runs server-side and is genuinely powerful for critical listeners. Sorriva's EQService covers practical tone control (bass, treble, loudness, balance) via Sonos HTTP and BluOS APIs. Real honest gap: Sorriva doesn't do server-side DSP and isn't trying to. The target user wants great sound, not signal processing configuration.
Hi-res audio delivery
Streaming or local files above CD quality (24-bit/96kHz+)
 ✓ RAAT bit-perfect ◎ Via Connect Parity*
Roon delivers bit-perfect hi-res via RAAT to Roon Ready DACs — genuinely impressive. Sorriva delivers 24/192 via Qobuz Connect and Tidal Connect to Bluesound hardware; 24/48 to Sonos via native API; lossless CD quality on AirPlay 2 paths. Hi-res requires the right endpoint hardware — Sorriva is honest about this with a per-zone Signal Path indicator. *Parity on Bluesound; Sonos caps at 24/48 regardless of app. "Nobody gets lossy. Everyone gets lossless. Hi-res requires the right hardware."
Zone grouping — any combination
Group any mix of zones into named rooms or scenes
 ~ Per-protocol only ↑ Best quality or everywhere Ours
Roon zones are siloed by protocol — you cannot group a Sonos zone with a RAAT zone or an AirPlay zone. Getting everything playing together requires understanding protocol types and manually reconfiguring endpoints. Most users never figure it out. Sorriva abstracts this into one decision the user actually cares about: best quality or everywhere. Best quality groups within ecosystems using native APIs — Sonos zones at 24/48, Bluesound zones at 24/192. Everywhere groups everything via AirPlay 2 fallback — whole house, lossless CD quality, one tap. Quality badge shown on every group before you press play. No protocol knowledge required. "Best quality when you want it. Everywhere when you want that."

Positioning summary vs Roon

Roon proves the market. Audiophiles will pay a premium for a beautiful, intelligent music layer — they already do. But Roon's architecture is its ceiling: a $130/year product that requires a server, locks you to certified hardware, and treats your phone as an afterthought. Those aren't bugs they can easily fix — they're foundational to how RAAT and the Roon Core model works.

Sorriva's answer isn't "Roon, but cheaper." It's "what Roon would have built if it had been designed for the App Store era." No server. Zone grouping via native Sonos and BluOS APIs — the same layer Roon uses. Hi-res 24/192 to Bluesound hardware via Qobuz Connect and Tidal Connect. Spotify and Apple Music inside the same app as your local FLAC collection. A first-class iPhone and Apple TV experience. Deriva instead of a filter panel. Household pricing instead of a single-user annual fee.

On audio quality: Sorriva delivers lossless on every path. 24/192 to Bluesound via Connect protocols. 24/48 to Sonos via native API — the same ceiling Roon hits on Sonos. Lossless CD quality on mixed-hardware AirPlay 2 groups. Nobody gets lossy audio. The Signal Path indicator makes this visible and honest rather than hidden — because transparency about what your system is actually delivering is a feature, not a disclaimer.

On zone grouping: Roon's protocol rigidity means you cannot group across ecosystems at any quality level — a Sonos zone and a Bluesound zone simply cannot play together in Roon. Sorriva solves both ends: native API grouping within ecosystems for best quality, AirPlay 2 fallback for whole-house "everywhere" mode. One decision, clearly labelled, no protocol knowledge required. Roon presents this limitation as audiophile purity — Sorriva's users experience it as genuine flexibility.

Roon's existence is validation. Its limitations are the product brief.
